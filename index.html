Campus Life

school hostel

Was established in 1978

There are separate boarding houses for boys and girls having bed strength of 200. Each house is placed under the supervision of a Housemaster / Housemistress. There is a matron in residence for each house to care for the well being of the students. Modern, tiled both Indian and western bathrooms are within the dormitories. The School has dispensed with the double decker beds and has provided the students with individual beds, spacious cupboards, study tables, with drawers and bookshelves. This helps create the necessary environment so very essential for a healthy teaching – learning experience. Each dorm has been provided with a common room and recreation area which provide the students the opportunity of relaxing and entertaining themselves after a hard day’s work.

school library

Was build in 1980

It is difficult to visualize a School without a library cum resource centre. The School possess a well – stocked library with over two thousand volumes which include some great names like Will Durant , CDM Kettlebee , William Shakespeare , Thomas Hardy , George Orwell , J. Krishnamurty , Munshi Premchand , Jaishankar Prasad , Gandhi , Nehru and others .The resource centre forms an integral part of the students’ learning rocess. Students are allotted ample time and opportunity to make use of the resource centre. They are provided with the facility of downloading information vital to their courses and for general awareness. At the same time it is expected that the children respect the resource centre and its working system.

school infirmary

Was build in 1982

The School provides for a six bed hospital and an infirmary which is well equipped to cater to minor injuries and sickness. This is looked after by a nursing attendant and a visiting doctor. In case of emergencies the School has tie ups with numerous specialist nursing homes in the city. The resident nursing sister is available round the clock. Regular medical check – ups are held to monitor the general health of the children. Home visits in case of serious illness is purely at the discretion of the School.

school Activites

Curriculum
A dynamic and rigorous yet liberal academic programme which aims at engaging students minds in all disciplines leading to excellence is the cornerstone of the School academic philosophy. Here the children are exposed not only to Shakespeare but also to Paulo (The Alchemist) and J. K Rowling (Harry Potter). Teachings of J. Krishnamurty, Dr. Rajinder Prasad, Nehru and Gandhi form an integral part of the curriculum. The School has successfully synchronized the goodness of the West with that of East (India).It is also our endeavour to initiate a programme which leads to enquiry, debate and research – all important processes that stimulate the acquisition of knowledge.
Evaluation
The School’s evaluation system does away with traditional percentage indicators; instead it has a general grade and efficiency indicators which enable the students to measure their performance - strength as well as weaknesses objectively as well as rationally in a non – competitive but group environment. Scores are determined on the basis of tests, classroom discussions, assignments, subject related quizzes and competitions and projects. Projects involve outside school surveys, visit to public libraries, PowerPoint and audio visual presentations.The students are also assessed for the following personality traits : Academic Motivation , Academic Creativity , Academic Self Discipline , Academic Growth Potential , Independence , Self Confidence , Leadership , Initiative , Originality , Sense of Responsibility , Sense of Humour , Etiquettes and Habits , Emotional Maturity , Concern for Others , Reactions to Setbacks , Reaction to Criticism , Respect for Facility and Respect from Peers.

What's new

  • Endurance Run-2020

    To encourage physical fitness and team spirit the School has a well defined games programme. Seasonal games include cricket, hockey, football, athletics and swimming. Minor games include basketball, tennis, volleyball, skating and which are played around the year.Mondays, Wednesdays and Fridays are reserved for games. All games played culminate into house matches with points being allotted to the winning house. A Cock House Cup is awarded, at the end of the term to the House which has secured the maximum points. Regular gymnastics, boxing and endurance building training programme are held throughout the year.

  • about meals

    The School lays a lot of emphasis on hygienic and nutritious food necessary for a growing child. For this the school has taken the services of a dietician who not only supervises the food but also lays the menu. The school provides vegetarian and non – vegetarian food. Mutton / chicken / egg curry is provided four times a week. For vegetarians it is paneer / mushrooms which is served four times a week. Every meal is accompanied by a fruit or a dessert/ ice – cream. Dal, rice, chapattis, vegetables, salads and curd are a regular feature.
